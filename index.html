Rozz Williams

born Roger Alan Painter

Here's a time line of Rozz Williams:

  • November 6, 1963 - Born in Pomona, California in a strict Southern Baptist family.
  • around 1979 - Began to perform in different bands ('The Crawlers', 'No', 'The Upsetters', 'The Asexuals', 'Daucus Karota').
  • October 1979 - Together with Jay (John) Albert form Christian Death (with James McGearty and George Belanger). The band broke up temporarily in 1981, and later the same year it was restarted by Williams (with addition of Rikk Agnew).
  • 1983 - Williams formed a new band under the Christian Death name with former members of Pompeii 99 (Williams as frontman, Valor Kand on guitars, Gitane Demone on keyboards and backing vocals, Constance Smith on bass and David Glass on drums).
  • April 1985 - Rozz officially left Christian Death. Williams released various material under different project names following his departure from the band.
  • 1987 - Rozz and Eva O (Eva Ortiz) marriage. They formed 'Shadow Project' together.
  • 1992 - Williams recorded two new Christian Death studio albums entitled 'The Path of Sorrows' and 'The Rage of Angels'
  • 1993 - Christian Death regrouped for the last show with Williams. Shadow Project had come to an end.
  • 1995 - Demone and Williams recorded the album 'Dream Home Heartache' together in Belgium.
  • 1997 - Rozz paired up with Eva O to record the final Shadow Project album, 'From the Heart'. He also recorded Wound of Exit, his last solo CD as Premature Ejaculation.
  • early 1998 - Williams co-directed and scored an experimental psychological horror silent short film 'Pig'.
  • April 1, 1998 - Williams hanged himself in his West Hollywood apartment, at the age of 34. He had left a rose on the coffee table in the living room, along with several items, including The Hanged Man tarot card, but no note.
